  • Indoor/outdoor flexibility

Cons

Mario-Themed Activities

Keep the kids entertained with these fun Mario-inspired activities!

Super Mario Obstacle Course

$40-50

Create a series of obstacles representing Mario game elements for kids to navigate through.

Materials Needed:

  • Cardboard boxes painted as blocks ($10)
  • Hula hoops for coin jumping ($15)
  • Pool noodles for pipes ($10)
  • Balloon "Goombas" to knock over ($5)

Instructions:

Kids navigate through obstacles, collecting "coins" (gold chocolate coins) along the way. Perfect for 4-year-olds with adult supervision.

Mario Kart Races

$35-45

Create a simple race track with cones or chalk where kids can race like in Mario Kart.

Materials Needed:

  • Traffic cones or sidewalk chalk ($10)
  • Ride-on cars or decorated scooters (can use existing toys)
  • Character hats for racers ($20)
  • Checkered flags ($5)

Instructions:

Set up a small race course where kids can take turns racing. This aligns perfectly with Rachel's vision of kids racing in little cars at Freewheel Bike Park.

Character Dress-Up Station

$45-55

Create a station with Mario character costume elements for dress-up and photos.

Materials Needed:

  • Red and green hats ($15)
  • Fake mustaches ($8)
  • Princess crowns ($12)
  • Props for photos ($10)

Instructions:

Kids can dress up as their favorite character for photos. This is a great activity for 4-year-olds who love pretend play.

Yoshi Egg Hunt

$45-55

Hide plastic eggs around the party area for kids to find.

Materials Needed:

  • Green plastic eggs ($10)
  • Small prizes or candy to fill eggs ($15)
  • Decorative basket for each child ($20)

Instructions:

Kids search for hidden "Yoshi eggs" containing treats. This is perfect for 4-year-olds who love scavenger hunts.

Craft Station

$25-35

Set up a table with craft supplies for Mario-themed crafts.

Materials Needed:

  • Pre-cut Mario masks templates ($5)
  • Craft sticks ($3)
  • Markers, glue, scissors ($10)
  • Star wand materials ($7)

Instructions:

Kids can create their own Mario masks or power-up wands. This is a great quiet activity for when kids need a break from more active play.

Recommended Activities

For a 4-year-old's party, we recommend choosing 2-3 activities from the list above. The Mario Kart Races would be especially appropriate given Rachel's vision for the race cars at Freewheel Bike Park.

Total Activities Budget

  • All Activities: $190-240

Mario-Themed Food & Drinks

Delight your guests with these themed treats!

Super Star Cookies

$16.75

Star-shaped sugar cookies with yellow frosting, just like the power stars in Mario games!

Ingredients:

  • 3 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
  • Pinch of salt
  • 1 cup (2 sticks) unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1 egg
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Yellow sanding sugar (for decoration)

Kid-Friendly Adaptations:

  • Pre-measure ingredients for 4-year-olds to pour and mix
  • Let kids help sprinkle the yellow sugar
  • Use pre-made sugar cookie dough to simplify the process

Power-Up Fruit Cups

$31.00

Colorful fruit cups that give kids a healthy "power-up" during the party!

Ingredients:

  • Assorted colorful fruits (strawberries, kiwi, pineapple, blueberries, grapes)
  • Clear plastic cups
  • Optional toppings: whipped cream, granola, chocolate chips
  • Gold coins (chocolate) for decoration

Kid-Friendly Adaptations:

  • Pre-cut all fruits for safety
  • Use mustache stickers on cups for a Mario/Luigi theme
  • Add white chocolate chips to strawberry garnishes to make them look like Piranha Plants

Mario Pizza Station

$34.00

Let kids build their own mini pizzas - perfect for Italian plumbers!

Ingredients:

  • Refrigerated biscuit dough (for individual pizza crusts)
  • Squeezable pizza sauce
  • Shredded mozzarella cheese
  • Pepperoni
  • Assorted toppings (bell peppers, olives, mushrooms)

Kid-Friendly Adaptations:

  • Use squeezable sauce bottles to minimize mess
  • Pre-portion cheese and toppings for easier handling
  • Suggest Mario character designs (red pepperoni for Mario's hat, olive eyes, etc.)

Luigi Lemonade & Mario Punch

$30.00

Colorful themed drinks that kids will love!

Luigi Lemonade Ingredients:

  • 1 gallon lemonade
  • Green food coloring
  • 2 liters ginger ale
  • 46 oz pineapple juice (1 large can)

Mario Punch Ingredients:

  • 1 gallon fruit punch
  • 2 liters lemon-lime soda
  • 46 oz pineapple juice (1 large can)

Kid-Friendly Adaptations:

  • Use red cups for Mario Punch and green cups for Luigi Lemonade
  • Add fun straws with Mario character toppers
  • Create printable drink labels

Planning Timeline

Follow this timeline to ensure everything is ready for the big day!

4 Weeks Before (Mid-April)
  • Confirm venue reservation for May 10th
  • Send invitations to guests
  • Order/purchase basic decorations
  • Book entertainment (bounce house, face painter, etc.)
2 Weeks Before (Late April)
  • Confirm RSVPs
  • Purchase non-perishable food items
  • Gather materials for activities
  • Order cake or purchase cake ingredients
1 Week Before (Early May)
  • Confirm all vendor bookings
  • Create detailed shopping list for perishables
  • Prepare any DIY decorations
  • Plan party day timeline
1-2 Days Before
  • Purchase perishable food items
  • Bake cookies and prepare any make-ahead food
  • Set up decorations that can be done in advance
  • Confirm final headcount
Party Day (May 10th)
  • Set up remaining decorations
  • Prepare food stations
  • Welcome entertainment vendors
  • Enjoy the party!
